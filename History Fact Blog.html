30-Interesting Facts About History And Discoveries You Should Know



#Fact.1 -

A Hungarian named Ladislo Biro invented the first ballpoint pen in 1938.





#Fact.2 -

Every major country, including the United States, abandoned the gold standard during the Great Depression. In fact, leaving the gold standard was a predictor of a country's economic severity and the length of time for its recovery. However, Herbert Hoover argued that abandoning the gold standard was the first step toward "communism, fascism, socialism, statism, and a planned economy.'.





#Fact.3 -

In 560 B.C., the Lydians introduced the first gold coin, a naturally occurring amalgam of gold and silver called electrum. Herodotus criticizes the materialism of the Lydians, who also were the first to open permanent retail shops. When the Lydians were captured by the Persians in 546 B.C., the use of gold coins began to spread.





#Fact.4 -

In 1971, the national debt was $75 million. In 2010, the debt rate rose that much once an hour.





#Fact.5 -

The movie Cleopatra cost $44 million to make in 1963. With inflation taken into account, the same movie would cost $300 million to make today.





#Fact.6 -

The first general credit cards were made from paper and had a limit of $300.





#Fact.7 -

The dollar has lost 21% of its purchasing power in the last decade.





#Fact.8 -

Gold is chemically inert, which also explains why it never rusts and does not cause skin irritation. If gold jewellery irritates the skin, it is likely that the gold was mixed with some other metal.





#Fact.9 -

The first novel was ever written on a typewriter: Tom Sawyer.





#Fact.10 -

The U.S. government borrows approximately $5 billion every business day.





#Fact.11 -

The first TONKA truck was made in 1947.





#Fact.12 -

People who lost their homes often lived in what were called "Hoovervilles," or shantytowns, that were named after President Herbert Hoover. There was also "Hoover Stew" (food dished out in soup kitchens), "Hoover Blankets" (newspapers that served as blankets), "Hoover Hogs" (jackrabbits used as food), and "Hoover Wagons" (broken cars that were pulled by mules).





#Fact.13 -

The Romans made their coins in the temple of Juno Moneta, the goddess of marriage and women. From the name Moneta, we get our words "mint" and "money." The word "money" now refers to anything that can be used to pay for goods and services or to pay a debt.





#Fact.14 -

A trillion $10 bills end to end would wrap around the globe 380 times. This would still not be enough pay off the national debt.





#Fact.15 -

The first words that Thomas A. Edison said into the phonograph were, "Mary had a little lamb.".





#Fact.16 -

Due to its high value, most gold discovered throughout history is still in circulation. However, it is thought that 80% of the world's gold is still in the ground.





#Fact.17 -

Cleopatra married two of her younger brothers Ptolemy XIII and Ptolemy XIV.





#Fact.18 -

The first coins with their value printed on them were minted around 600 B.C. in ancient Lydia (modern-day Turkey). The coins were made of electrum, which is an alloy of gold and silver.





#Fact.19 -

The first known contraceptive was a mixture of crocodile dung and honey, used by Egyptian women in 1850 B.C.





#Fact.20 -

Credit cards were first used in the 1920s. Hotels were the first to offer cards to their customers to pay for their hotel stays. Soon department stores and gas companies offered their own cards. All these cards, however, could be used only at the business that issued them. The Diner Card arrived in the 1950s and could be used at different restaurants and hotels. In 1951, some banks began issuing credit cards that could be used in different places.





#Fact.21 -

Ancient Egyptians believed that onions would keep evil spirits away.





#Fact.22 -

The most famous demonstration during the Great Depression was held by the "Bonus Army." It consisted largely of WWI veterans who requested financial bonuses that were scheduled to be given in 1945 to be paid instead in 1932. The U.S. Army was called in to disperse them.





#Fact.23 -

In the past, English pygg was a type of clay that was used to make jars and dishes that held money. The word eventually morphed into "piggy bank.'.





#Fact.24 -

Arthur Giblin was the inventor of the first "flushable" toilet.





#Fact.25 -

There have been many unusual forms of money throughout history, such as the cowry, which is the shell of a sea snail. They were colourful and durable, which made them popular money with civilizations around the world. The cowry was the longest and most widely used currency in history.





#Fact.26 -

During the fourteenth century, drinking molten gold and crushed emeralds were used as a treatment for the bubonic plague.





#Fact.27 -

On September 9, 1950, dubbed laughter was used for the first time on television. It was used for the sitcom "The Hank McCune Show.".





#Fact.28 -

A famous bullfighter, Lagarijo, killed 4,867 bulls in the 19th century.





#Fact.29 -

Walter Cavanagh of California is known as "Mr Plastic Fantastic." He has more than 13,000 credit cards, the largest collection in the world. He keeps his cards in the world's largest wallet, that is 250 feet long.





#Fact.30 -

Alexander Hamilton was the first Secretary of the Treasury in the United States. To honour his contributions, his picture appeared on American money starting in the Civil War. Today his portrait is still on the $10 bill.
